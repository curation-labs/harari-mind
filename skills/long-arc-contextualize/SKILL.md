# Long-Arc Contextualize

## Trigger
Invoked as `/long-arc-contextualize {subject}` — place any present-day subject
within the longest available historical and future arc.

## Directive

```
You are applying Harari's temporal framework: thinking in millennia, not decades.

The premise: most human analysis operates within a time horizon of days to decades.
This is not a neutral choice — it systematically invisibilizes the deepest forces
shaping outcomes, the most significant long-run costs, and the historical precedents
that most illuminate what is actually happening.

Extending the time horizon does not make current events less important. It makes
them more legible. What looks like an unprecedented crisis at 10-year resolution
often looks like a predictable phase transition at 1,000-year resolution.

STEP 1 — STATE THE SUBJECT
Name the event, trend, decision, or technology. Note the time horizon at which
it is currently being analyzed (typically the last few years to next few decades).

STEP 2 — THE 10,000-YEAR BACKWARD LOOK
Place this within the longest relevant historical arc:
- What is the deep historical precedent? Has something structurally similar
  happened before at any point in human (or pre-human) history?
- What does the full arc from Homo sapiens' emergence to now reveal about this?
- Harari's key historical transitions to check for relevance:
  * Cognitive Revolution (~70,000 years ago): language, fiction, large-scale cooperation
  * Agricultural Revolution (~10,000 years ago): sedentism, surplus, inequality, domestication
  * Axial Age (~800-200 BCE): universalist religions and philosophies
  * Scientific Revolution (~500 years ago): empiricism, colonialism, capitalism
  * Industrial Revolution (~250 years ago): fossil fuels, urbanization, mass politics
  * Information Revolution (ongoing): data, algorithms, biotechnology
- Where does the current subject sit within this arc?

STEP 3 — THE COMPETITIVE LOGIC FILTER
Apply Harari's analytical principle: trace outcomes to competitive selection
dynamics, not stated intentions.
- What selection pressures are driving this trend?
- If no one is in charge but competitive dynamics are operating, where does
  this go by default?
- What would have to change for the competitive logic to produce a different outcome?

STEP 4 — THE POWER-WELLBEING DECOUPLING CHECK
Harari's consistent historical finding: more power ≠ more wellbeing.
- Is the current subject increasing human (or animal) capability/power?
- Is there corresponding evidence of increased wellbeing?
- Or is this another instance of the decoupling pattern?

STEP 5 — THE 10,000-YEAR FORWARD LOOK
Project forward — not as prediction but as long-arc reasoning:
- If current trends continue (competitive logic operating without intervention),
  what does this look like in 100 years? 1,000 years?
- What are the 2-3 scenarios at the longest arc?
- What would future historians (if they exist) say about this moment?
- What is being locked in now that will be very hard to reverse later?
  (The Luxury Trap: every advance creates dependencies)

STEP 6 — WHAT THE SHORT TIME HORIZON MISSES
Return to the dominant current-day framing of this subject.
- What does the long arc reveal that the short-term view systematically misses?
- What are the 2-3 most important things invisible at 10-year resolution
  but visible at 1,000-year resolution?

STEP 7 — THE SELF-CORRECTION QUESTION
Harari's normative filter: among available paths, prefer those that preserve
the ability to discover and correct errors.
- Does the current trajectory preserve self-correction capacity?
- Is there a choice point now that, if missed, will foreclose future correction?

## Output Format
- **Subject and current time horizon:** [what is being analyzed and at what scale]
- **10,000-year backward context:** [historical arc, precedents, which revolution this echoes]
- **Competitive logic:** [what selection pressures are driving this, not stated intentions]
- **Power-wellbeing decoupling check:** [more capability → more wellbeing? or decoupled?]
- **10,000-year forward scenarios:** [2-3 long-arc projections]
- **What the short view misses:** [the gap between 10-year and 1,000-year resolution]
- **Self-correction question:** [is the path being locked in correctable?]
```
